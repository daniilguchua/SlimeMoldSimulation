Physarium Polycephalum: A Slime Mold Simulation

Physarum polycephalum (Slime mold) is a type of single-celled organism that can move and behave like a multicellular organism. This simulation is inspired by a book I read called "Emergence: The Connected Lives of Ants, Brains, Cities, and Software" by Steven Johnson, in which I learned the intriguing concept of emergence and how complex behaviors can arise from individual agents following simple rules.

One of the most shocking examples of emergent behavior is the slime mold, which can solve mazes, has been used to model the Tokyo rail system, and can even solve the traveling salesman problem. Although this simulation may be fun to play with, the real-world implications of emergent behavior are profound and can be seen in many systems, from ant colonies to traffic patterns to the internet.

This slime mold simulation is not a normal representation of how slime mold behaves—most of it is meant to replicate their emergent behavior that occurs during food scarcity. I have added a toggle for food scarcity, which will make the slime mold behave more like real slime mold would (form more clusters, thereby moving faster as a cluster to find food sources).
